Franklin's Newest Park "The Pearl" Is Taking Shape

Franklin is adding a significant new green space to its parks system. The city has announced plans for a new park called The Pearl, currently on approximately a three-year development timeline, with early site work already underway.

The project includes the construction of Robinson Lake along the park's northern edge , a new body of water that will feature a dock and be stocked for fishing. Lake construction is expected to wrap up within the next 18 months, making it one of the earlier visible milestones in the broader park development.

## What We Know So Far

Details on the full scope of The Pearl are still emerging as the project progresses, but the lake component alone signals the city's ambition for the space. A stocked fishing lake with dock access would be a meaningful addition to Franklin's already strong outdoor infrastructure , complementing the Harpeth River corridor, the city's greenway trail system, and established parks like Pinkerton and Jim Warren.

The name itself , The Pearl , suggests a centerpiece destination rather than a standard neighborhood park, consistent with how Franklin has approached its most recent civic investments.

## Why It Matters for Relocating Families

Franklin's parks and trails system is one of the city's most underappreciated assets and a consistent highlight for families who move here from more densely developed markets. The Pearl represents continued investment in that infrastructure at a moment when Franklin's population growth is accelerating demand for quality outdoor space.
